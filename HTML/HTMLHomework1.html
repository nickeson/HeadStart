String Gauge Basics

  • There are various string gauges commonly in use for stringing your guitar. Typically they range from .010 to .013 for a high 'E' string, and the rest of the set is generally scaled based on the gauge of the high 'E' string.
  • Hybrid sets are also available which use lighter gauges on the upper strings and heavier gauges on the lower strings. These are specialty and not common.

String Gauge In Depth

  • Lighter gauges allow you to have lower action on the guitar
  • Heavier gauges require you to have higher action to avoid fret-buzz
  • Lighter gauge strings have a 'thinner' tone
  • Heavier gauge strings have a 'fatter' tone
  • Lighter gauge strings are easier to bend and play
  • Heavier gauge strings are harder to bend and play

Conclusions

String gauge is a matter of personal preference and pain tolerance vs. tone
